Meet the HAIS Club Staff.

Gaoyong Yang-Vang

President

Gaoyong Yang-Vang, primarily known as Gao, was born in Fresno, CA, but has been raised all around the Central Valley. She is in her first year in Fresno State's MFA program. She studies poetry and writes about the instability of her childhood and attempts to understand her own past without familial roots and exploring non-traditional identities of a Hmong woman. On her writing, friend and mentor Anthony Cody, says Gao “explores Hmongness in fresh, unexpected ways that ask readers to think beyond the roles and tropes often depicted in popular culture of Hmong.”

Tony Vang

Vice President

Tony Vang was born and raised in Fresno, and he is quick to always proudly claim that he is from Southeast Fresno. He originally had no interest in writing, or college for that matter, but after taking a poetry writing class, he discovered that he enjoyed writing more than anything else college had to offer. As a result, he remained in the writing community for a handful of years, practicing and reading poetry until this very day. Tony mostly writes love poems, having discovered his affinity for the genre after reading Pablo Neruda’s “Love Sonnet XVII”.

Mai Der Vang

Faculty Advisor

Mai Der Vang is the author of Afterland (Graywolf Press, 2017), winner of the 2016 Walt Whitman Award of the Academy of American Poets, longlisted for the 2017 National Book Award in Poetry, and a finalist for the 2018 Kate Tufts Discovery Award. The recipient of a Lannan Literary Fellowship, she served as a Visiting Writer at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago. Her poetry has appeared in Poetry, Tin House, the American Poetry Review, among other journals and anthologies. Her essays have been published in the New York Times, the Washington Post, and elsewhere. Born and raised in Fresno, California, she earned degrees from the University of California, Berkeley and Columbia University. She teaches in the MFA Creative Writing Program at Fresno State.

Lee Lee

Treasurer

Lee Lee was born and raised in Fresno, California. She is a second-year fiction student in Fresno State's MFA Program in Creative Writing. Her current works seek to understand the real and imagined inner worlds of being a Hmong woman, gender roles and flying chickens included. She spends too many late nights reading manhwas, listening to sad love songs, and drawing K-pop fanart.

Laura Thor

Secretary

Laura Thor was born in Stockton and raised in Fresno, CA. She is in her last year of her undergraduate studies for English with the focus of creative writing. Reading has always been her source of inspiration and escape whenever the world around her felt tense and hopeless. As she got older, her interest towards writing sparked from personal narratives that inspired her to understand her identity as a Hmong American. This caused her to change her major from pre-nursing to English, which was her first step towards pursuing what she genuinely wanted to do. Her current goal as a young writer and scholar is to further expose herself to Asian American literature in order to learn more about the patterns of cultural migration.

Maiyang Lor

Social Media Manager

Maiyang Lor was born and raised in Appleton, Wisconsin. She then spent most of her high school years in Florida with the gators before graduating in Fresno, California. She is also a recent Fresno State graduate with a BA degree in English Education and a minor in Art (Graphic Design). During her leisure time, Mai is either spoiling her cats, writing fictional stories, or playing PC games with her fiancé.
